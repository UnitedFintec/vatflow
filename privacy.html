2 What Data We Collect

2.1 Account Information

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Password (stored as a one-way cryptographic hash — never in plain text)
  • Company or business name
  • Business address
  • Phone number (optional)

2.2 Tax and Financial Data

  • VAT Registration Number (VRN)
  • Company registration number
  • VAT return figures (Boxes 1–9)
  • Sales transaction records (invoice number, customer name, date, net amount, VAT amount, VAT rate)
  • Purchase transaction records (supplier name, date, net amount, VAT amount)
  • VAT accounting scheme information (Standard, Flat Rate, Cash Accounting, Annual, Retail)
  • Flat Rate Scheme percentage
  • VAT period dates

2.3 HMRC OAuth Tokens

  • HMRC OAuth 2.0 access tokens (encrypted at rest)
  • HMRC OAuth 2.0 refresh tokens (encrypted at rest)
  • These tokens allow the service to communicate with HMRC on your behalf. They do not contain your HMRC Government Gateway password.

2.4 Technical and Usage Data

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device type and operating system
  • Pages visited and features used within VATflow
  • Date and time of access
  • Error logs

2.5 Payment Data

  • Subscription plan selected
  • Billing history (amount, date, status)
  • Payment card details are not stored by us — they are handled by Stripe (PCI-DSS compliant) and GoCardless

3 How We Collect Your Data

  • Directly from you — when you register, enter VAT figures, upload an Excel template, or contact us
  • From HMRC's MTD API — when you authorise VATflow via OAuth 2.0, we retrieve your VAT obligations and related data from HMRC on your behalf
  • From accounting software — if you connect Xero, Sage, FreeAgent, QuickBooks, or Anna Money via their APIs, we receive transaction data from those services
  • Automatically — technical data such as IP address, browser type, and usage data is collected when you use the service

6 Data Sharing

We share your data only in the following circumstances:

6.1 HMRC (His Majesty's Revenue and Customs)

Your VAT return data and associated fraud prevention headers are transmitted to HMRC via their Making Tax Digital API. This is the core function of the service and is required by law. HMRC's own privacy notice applies to data they receive: gov.uk/hmrc/privacy.

6.2 Payment Processors

  • Stripe — processes card payments for subscriptions. Stripe is PCI-DSS compliant. Stripe's privacy policy: stripe.com/privacy
  • GoCardless — processes Direct Debit payments. GoCardless is FCA authorised. GoCardless privacy policy: gocardless.com/privacy

6.3 Accounting Software (if connected)

If you connect Xero, Sage, FreeAgent, QuickBooks, or Anna Money, we receive transaction data from those services. We act as a Data Processor in relation to data received from those platforms.

6.4 Infrastructure Providers

  • Email delivery service (for transactional emails only)

6.5 Legal Requirements

We may disclose data if required by law, court order, or to protect the rights and safety of users or the public.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party.

8 Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • All data transmitted using TLS 1.2 or higher encryption (HTTPS)
  • Passwords stored using industry-standard one-way hashing (bcrypt)
  • HMRC OAuth tokens encrypted at rest using AES-256
  • Access to personal data restricted to authorised personnel only
  • Regular security assessments of our infrastructure
  • UK-based server infrastructure
  • Automatic session expiry
  • All API communications with HMRC include mandatory fraud prevention headers as required by law

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and affected individuals without undue delay, as required by UK GDPR Article 33–34.

11 HMRC & MTD Specific Provisions

11.1 Fraud Prevention Headers

HMRC requires all MTD software providers to submit fraud prevention header data with every API call. This is a legal requirement under the Finance Act 2021 and HMRC's terms of use. The data transmitted includes connection method, device identifier, timezone, and software version information. This data is transmitted to HMRC and is governed by HMRC's privacy notice.

11.2 Digital Records

MTD regulations (SI 2018/261) require that digital records of VAT transactions are maintained for 6 years. VATflow stores your digital records in compliance with this requirement. You may export your records at any time from the Digital Records section of the application.

11.3 HMRC OAuth Authorisation

When you connect VATflow to HMRC via the "Authorise with HMRC Government Gateway" function, you are redirected to HMRC's own Government Gateway. VATflow never sees or stores your Government Gateway username or password. We only receive and store the OAuth access token and refresh token that HMRC issues after you grant authority. You can revoke this authority at any time via your HMRC online account.

11.4 Tax Agent Use

If you are a tax agent using VATflow to file on behalf of your clients, you are responsible for ensuring your clients have been informed that their VAT data is processed by VATflow and for obtaining any necessary consents. A Data Processing Agreement is available on request at contact@unitedfintec.com.
